# Logo Design Brief

## ForgeOrchestra Logo System

---

## Core Principles

### Do's
✅ Minimal and architectural
✅ Monoline geometric construction
✅ Refined wordmark with compact emblem
✅ Balanced symmetry
✅ Disciplined geometry
✅ Soft intelligent motion cues

### Don'ts
❌ Busy symbols with pipes, gears, nodes, music notes, AI swirls
❌ Cyberpunk aesthetics
❌ Gamer-ish styling
❌ Overly industrial clichés
❌ Cartoon-ish abstraction

---

## Logo Directions

### Direction 1: Monoline Geometric Emblem

**Concept:** A balanced symbol made from curved and straight segments representing central command and branching systems.

**Characteristics:**
- Single continuous line weight (2px)
- Combines arcs and straight segments
- Suggests hub-and-spoke orchestration
- Compact, scalable

**Visual References:**
- Intersecting orbital paths
- Branching network nodes
- Conductor baton motion trails

---

### Direction 2: Folded Frame Mark

**Concept:** A shape that feels like a forged, bent, precision-formed structure.

**Characteristics:**
- Layered geometric planes
- Suggests engineered utility
- Premium, machined appearance
- Subtle depth through layering

**Visual References:**
- Bent metal frames
- Layered circuit boards
- Architectural blueprints

---

### Direction 3: Orbital Grid Mark

**Concept:** A central core with controlled paths around it.

**Characteristics:**
- Central focal point
- Orbiting pathways
- Implies scheduling, routing, intelligence
- Modular, expandable

**Visual References:**
- Planetary orbits
- PCB traces
- Scheduling Gantt charts abstracted

---

### Direction 4: Wordmark-First Identity

**Concept:** The wordmark carries the brand 80% of the time; symbol is secondary.

**Characteristics:**
- Exceptionally strong typography
- "ForgeOrchestra" as primary mark
- Compact emblem for favicons, avatars
- Clean, neutral grotesk typeface

**Typography Requirements:**
- Neutral but premium
- Geometric without feeling cold
- Highly legible
- Contemporary
- Slightly condensed or tightly controlled spacing

---

## Wordmark Specifications

### Format
```
ForgeOrchestra
```

**NOT:**
- FORGEORCHESTRA (all caps)
- Forge Orchestra (split unless editorial)

### Product Family Convention
```
ForgeOrchestra     (parent)
ForgeCalendar      (time & scheduling)
ForgePipeline      (workflow & automation)
ForgeDisplay       (presentation & dashboards)
ForgeOpenClaw      (agent orchestration)
```

### Typography
- **Weight:** 500-600 (medium to semi-bold)
- **Tracking:** -0.02em (slightly tight)
- **Case:** Title case (ForgeOrchestra)
- **Style:** Neutral grotesk / neo-grotesk

---

## Emblem Usage

### Sizes
- **Large:** 64px+ (header logos, hero sections)
- **Medium:** 32px (navigation, cards)
- **Small:** 24px (favicons, app icons)
- **Micro:** 16px (browser tabs, notifications)

### Clear Space
Minimum clear space = 1x emblem height on all sides.

### Backgrounds
- Primary: Dark obsidian (`#07090D`)
- Secondary: Slate glass (`#161D2E`)
- Accent: Product-specific gradient (subtle)

---

- `logo-icon-32.png` (app icon)

---

## Logo Don'ts

❌ Stretch or distort proportions
❌ Add effects (drop shadows, bevels, gradients beyond brand spec)
❌ Rotate or flip
❌ Change colors outside brand palette
❌ Place on busy backgrounds without clear space
❌ Use low-resolution versions
❌ Recreate from memory (always use source files)

## Review Checklist

Before approving logo:

- [ ] Minimal and architectural
- [ ] Works at small sizes (16px)
- [ ] Works in monochrome
- [ ] Works in brand accent colors
- [ ] Clear space defined
- [ ] Vector source files delivered
- [ ] PNG exports at all required sizes
- [ ] No busy elements (gears, pipes, notes)
- [ ] Feels premium, not gimmicky
- [ ] Wordmark legible and well-spaced

---

**The mark is minimal. The wordmark is strong. Together they orchestrate.**
